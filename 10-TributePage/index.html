Margaret Hamilton

When I took over, one of the bosses at the top said he had no doubt I could do the job but was worried the men working in the group might rebel. Well, they didn’t.

  • 1936 - Born August 17, in Paoli, Indiana
  • 1954 - Graduated from Hancock High School
  • 1955 - Studied mathematics at the University of Michigan in 1955 before transferring to Earlham College
  • 1958 - She earned a BA in mathematics with a minor in philosophy in 1958
  • 1961 - Started working on the Semi-Automatic Ground Environment (SAGE) Project at the MIT Lincoln Lab. SAGE was soon developed for military use in anti-aircraft air defense. Joined the Charles Stark Draper Laboratory at MIT, which worked on the Apollo Space Mission.
  • 1965 - In charge of all Command Module software, which was all the software for navigation and lunar landing guidance.
  • 1976 - Co-founded with Saydean Zeldin a company called Higher Order Software (HOS) to further develop ideas about error prevention and fault tolerance emerging from their experience at MIT working on the Apollo program.
  • 1986 - Founded Hamilton Technologies, Inc. in Cambridge, Massachusetts. The company was developed around the Universal Systems Language (USL) and its associated automated environment, the 001 Tool Suite, based on her paradigm of development before the fact for systems design and software development.
  • 1986 - Received the Augusta Ada Lovelace Award by the Association for Women in Computing.
  • 2016 - Received the Presidential Medal of Freedom from Barack Obama, the highest civilian honor in the United States.
